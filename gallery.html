Great Ocean Road

It is a coastal road in the southwest of Victoria on the east coast of Australia. There are plenty of stops along the way, including Torquay & Bells Beach, Loch Ard Gorge, London Arch/ Bridge, and the most classic twelve The Twelve Apostles. Length: 243 km Constructed: March 18, 1922 Opened: November 26, 1932

Moonlight Wildlife Conservation Park

Moonlight Wildlife Conservation Park is a private small zoo. On the road, visitors will encounter baby kangaroos or various small animals unique to Australia. Location: 550 Tyabb-Tooradin Rd, Pearcedale VIC 3912 Transport: 10:00-17:00 (Night Tours are available in the evening, additional reservation is required)

Dandenong Ranges

The Dandenong Ranges region has several well-known cool-climate gardens, especially in the spring and fall. Visitors can stroll among large and small rhododendron bushes, camellias, and lilies at the National Rhododendron Gardens, as well as admire the artificial lake and surrounding landscape at the Alfred Nicholas Memorial Gardens, which is filled with a variety of trees, including almond-scented eucalyptus, Ginkgo, Maple and Liquidambar. Elevation: 633m Suitable for: picnic, hiking and riding

Brighton Beach

This is one of the most popular beaches in Melbourne. Except for the clear water, there are more than 80 huts neatly arranged here, all of which are similar in size and appearance, which originally built in the 1800s for people to change clothes on the beach. Because these huts are similar, the homeowners took great pains to paint their huts, such as the Australian flag, kangaroos, etc. Location: Victoria: south of the city between Elwood and Hampton and fronting Port Phillip

Museums

  • Melbourne Museum

    adult $15; Seniors $10; Child Free

    It is largest museum in the southern hemisphere, with seven main galleries, a children's gallery and a gallery of temporary exhibitions, known for its amazing diversity and interactivity of exhibits.

  • National Gallery of Victoria

    Free for everyone

    It is the largest art gallery in Australia. The most ingenious one is the entrance, which is a screen wall made of glass mirrors. Clear water continuously pours down along the glass mirrors, like the waterfall in the fairyland.

  • Australian Centre for Contemporary Art

    Free for everyone

    The stated mission of ACCA's is "do art differently - to challenge, connect and resonate". It's fun, young, with a little weird taste, but not boring, and there's always something surprised for you to discover.
